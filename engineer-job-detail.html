Fault Description
Subtransient Subtransient is first, and is associated with the largest currents
Transient Transient comes between subtransient and steady-state
Steady-state Steady-state occurs after all the transients have had time to settle
Positive-sequence A positive-sequence component, in which the phases are in the same order as the original system, i.e., a-b-c
Negative-sequence A negative-sequence component, in which the phases are in the opposite order as the original system, i.e., a-c-b
Zero-sequence A zero-sequence component, which is not truly a three-phase system, but instead all three phases are in phase with each other.
Line-to-line A short circuit between lines, caused by ionization of air, or when lines come into physical contact, for example due to a broken insulator.
Line-to-ground A short circuit between one line and ground, very often caused by physical contact, for example due to lightning or other storm damage.
Double line-to-ground Two lines come into contact with the ground (and each other), also commonly due to storm damage.
Deep-cycle batteries The prospective fault current of larger batteries, such as deep-cycle batteries used in stand-alone power systems, is often given by the manufacturer.
